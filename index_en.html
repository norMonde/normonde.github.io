What am I?

Hello, I'm Ronan, I love the sea and traveling! I've always thought about doing a big trip after my studies. My engineering degree in 2021 was the perfect transition to travel... But I didn't count on the COVID-19, which closed all borders at that time. So I decided to postpone my trip until 2024 by completing a doctorate in applied mathematics. Now I'm a doctor in applied mathematics on beach sediment dynamics. All I have to do now is prepare for my trip and get out of here! Here's hoping that monkeypox doesn't create a new global pandemic...

When I was growing up, I was lucky enough to have parents who loved to travel! Many of our trips were very important to me, and helped shape the individual I am today. But the most memorable experience for me was living in Japan from the age of 3 to 6. Despite my young age, I was both overwhelmed and amazed by this culture shock. Japan will remain in my heart forever, and I hope to go back there one day!

My last few years have helped me build my project. Indeed, spending days in an office can sometimes make you want to escape. Although my 8 years as a beach lifeguard allowed me to discover an exciting and above all outdoor profession, with a touch of adventure on each of our interventions, they also awakened in me a desire to go further in the adventure. My weekends, my crossings of France and my hitchhiking trips have awakened in me a desire to discover the world by hitchhiking. It's become essential to show that travel is not just about taking a plane ticket for 3 weeks and going back to the routine of work.

For me, this trip is a way of becoming an ambassador for hitchhiking and new travel, in the context of the ecological crisis.
